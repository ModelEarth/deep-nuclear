# Example Scenarios for Nuclear Desalination Calculator

This document provides example scenarios to demonstrate the calculator's capabilities and typical use cases.

## Scenario 1: Large Coastal Nuclear Plant (3000 MWth)

### Input Parameters
- **Reactor Thermal Power**: 3000 MWth
- **Waste Heat Utilization**: 67%
- **MED GOR**: 12
- **Number of Effects**: 8
- **Top Brine Temperature**: 70°C
- **Availability**: 90%
- **Water Price**: $0.50/m³
- **MED Capital Cost**: $1,500/m³/day
- **Retrofit Multiplier**: 1.2
- **Waste Tunnel**: 5 km
- **Fuel Tunnel**: 2 km
- **Boring Cost**: $50M/km

- **Annual Water Production**: ~328 million m³/year
- **Annual Revenue**: ~$164M/year
- **Total Project Cost**: ~$2.5B
- **Payback Period**: ~15 years

## Key Insights from Scenarios

### 1. Scale Matters
- Larger plants (3000 MWth) produce significantly more water
- Economies of scale improve cost per cubic meter
- Higher revenue potential offsets higher capital costs

### 2. Efficiency Impact
- Higher GOR (15 vs 9) increases production by ~33%
- More effects improve efficiency but increase capital cost
- Optimal balance depends on water price and capital constraints

### 3. Water Price Sensitivity
- $0.60/m³ vs $0.35/m³ can halve payback period
- Higher water prices make projects more attractive
- Regional water scarcity drives pricing

### 4. Infrastructure Costs
- Earthgrid boring costs: $140M-$350M depending on tunnel length
- Represents 5-15% of total project cost
- Can be funded through water sales revenue

### 5. Payback Period Range
- Typical range: 8-25 years
- Depends on:
  - Water price
  - Plant size
  - Efficiency configuration
  - Capital costs

## Recommendations

### For High Water Demand Regions
- Use Scenario 3 (High-Efficiency) configuration
- Maximize GOR and number of effects
- Target higher water prices ($0.50-$0.70/m³)
- Accept longer payback for higher production

### For Cost-Sensitive Projects
- Use Scenario 4 (Cost-Optimized) configuration
- Moderate GOR (9-10) and effects (6-8)
- Focus on lower capital costs
- Target competitive water pricing

### For Maximum Production
- Use Scenario 1 (Large Plant) configuration
- 3000+ MWth reactors
- High availability (90%+)
- Multiple MED units for redundancy

## Using the Calculator

1. **Start with Default Values**: The calculator loads with Scenario 1 defaults
2. **Adjust for Your Site**: Modify parameters based on:
   - Actual reactor thermal power
   - Local water pricing
   - Site-specific infrastructure needs
3. **Optimize Configuration**: Try different GOR and effect combinations
4. **Evaluate Economics**: Review NPV and payback period
5. **Generate Proposal**: Use the auto-generated summary for financing

## Notes

- All scenarios assume 20-year project lifetime for NPV calculations
- Discount rate: 10% (standard for infrastructure projects)
- O&M costs: 5% of total capital cost annually
- Water production adjusted for plant availability factor
- All costs in USD (adjust for local currency as needed)
